1. The idea

RUG OR RICHES is a press-your-luck crypto tapper built as a Telegram Mini App. It distills the exact emotion of degen trading — the thrill of riding a pump and the gut-punch of a rug — into a 10-second loop anyone can play, free, with no wallet.

You tap to pump a live $MOON chart. The longer you ride, the higher the multiplier — and the higher the rug risk. Cash out in time and you bank the gains; hold one tap too long and the chart rugs, wiping your unbanked position. It is self-aware crypto humour turned into a game of nerve.

3. Gameplay loop

  • Tap to pump — each tap stakes your bet and pushes the live price up.
  • Watch the heat — a multi-factor rug-risk meter climbs as you ride.
  • Cash out — bank your position before it rugs; clutch high-risk exits pay bonuses.
  • Manage risk — sell half, set auto-sell and stop-loss, ride win streaks, predict the next move.
  • Progress — bank $MOON to rank up, unlock upgrades and VIP, and grow your airdrop.

4. $MOON & Airdrop Points

The Game uses two in-game scores:

  • $MOON — your spendable in-game balance, earned by banking profitable rounds. Used for upgrades, VIP, and progression.
  • Airdrop Points — a separate Season 1 score earned through sustained, legitimate play and quests. Points cannot be wagered and are designed to reward real engagement over time.

Both are off-chain in-game points today. They are not a cryptocurrency and carry no guaranteed value.

7. Monetization

The Game is free to play. Optional purchases are made with Telegram Stars and grant in-game items only — never real-world value:

  • VIP tiers — earnings multipliers, higher bet caps, rug protection, daily lounge perks. Earnable with $MOON (the grind) or unlockable with Stars (skip the grind).
  • Cosmetic skins — collectible coin skins with rarity tiers; purely visual.
  • Boosts & convenience — energy refills, $MOON packs, season pass, and limited offers.

8. Fair play

The economy is server-authoritative. Your client never reports its own balance — it only requests actions (cash out, buy an upgrade, claim a reward), and the server validates and applies every balance and point change. The sync endpoint saves cosmetic settings only, so a tampered client cannot inject fake totals.

Each request carries Telegram's cryptographically signed identity, verified server-side, so every action is tied to a real account. Cash-out payouts are capped to the most a player's tier could plausibly win in a round, rewards are idempotent (each credits at most once), and implausible jumps are flagged — flagged accounts can be reset or made ineligible for rewards and the airdrop. Leaderboards read directly from the server database, so they only ever contain server-validated scores, and any future token allocation would come from a snapshot of that same authoritative data.

Provably-fair rounds are next on the roadmap: a commit-reveal scheme where the server publishes a hash of each round's secret seed before you play and reveals the seed after, so the rug outcome is locked in advance and independently verifiable — the house can't rug you for winning.

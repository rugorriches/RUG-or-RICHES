1. The idea

RUG OR RICHES is a press-your-luck crypto tapper built as a Telegram Mini App. It distills the exact emotion of degen trading — the thrill of riding a pump and the gut-punch of a rug — into a 10-second loop anyone can play, free, with no wallet.

You tap to pump a live $MOON chart. The longer you ride, the higher the multiplier — and the higher the rug risk. Cash out in time and you bank the gains; hold one tap too long and the chart rugs, wiping your unbanked position. It is self-aware crypto humour turned into a game of nerve.

📈 Real tension

A live market with buyers, sellers, fakeouts, soft rugs and hard rugs — outcomes you can read and time, not pure chance.

⚡ Instant loop

Every round is seconds long. "One more round" is the entire design — fast, screenshot-worthy, shareable.

🤝 Social by default

Crews, crew wars, referrals, chat, and global + regional leaderboards turn solo play into competition.

🪂 A reason to stay

Airdrop Points reward sustained, legitimate play across six pre-launch cycles — not one lucky run.

2. Full walkthrough — A to Z

This is the entire journey, not just the tap screen.

  1. Open the app. Launch from the Telegram bot (@RugorRichesBot) or the website Play button. No wallet, no signup — your Telegram identity is your account, verified server-side.
  2. Get your starting stack. New players begin with a small $MOON balance and starting Airdrop Points so you can play immediately.
  3. Set your bet. Choose a stake per round. Bigger bets mean bigger pumps and bigger swings.
  4. Tap to pump. Each tap stakes your bet and pushes the live price up, building your in-round position (the "pot").
  5. Read the heat. A multi-factor rug-risk meter climbs as you ride. Market events (FOMO surge, bear raid, whale accumulation, volatility) shift the odds in real time.
  6. Manage the position. Sell half to lock partial profit, set an auto-sell target, or set a stop-loss. Watch for the "DUMP INCOMING" warning.
  7. Cash out. Bank your position before it rugs. Clutch high-risk exits pay a bonus; ignore the warning and a hard rug wipes the unbanked pot.
  8. Bank builds your rank. Lifetime banked $MOON levels you from Shrimp to Megalodon, each unlocking energy, daily-bonus and VIP perks.
  9. Spend energy, refill it. Tapping is free, but a separate energy tank gates extended rushes; it regenerates over time, scales with rank, and can be refilled.
  10. Buy upgrades. Spend banked $MOON on eight permanent upgrades across Offense, Defense and Economy.
  11. Climb VIP. Earn or unlock four VIP tiers for earnings multipliers, rug protection and perks.
  12. Clear quests. Daily, weekly, monthly and VIP quests pay $MOON and feed your Airdrop Points.
  13. Claim daily rewards. Login streaks, a spin wheel, mystery boxes and a lucky draw keep you coming back.
  14. Go social. Join or create a crew, chat, fight weekly Crew Wars, and invite friends for referral rewards.
  15. Qualify for the airdrop. Sustained, legitimate play across the season builds your Airdrop Points toward qualification, Elite status, and a snapshot at the end of each cycle.
  16. Repeat across six cycles. Each pre-launch cycle is a fresh season with its own rising allocation — the earlier you start, the more cycles you can stack.

5. Crews & Crew Wars

Crews are player teams. Join an existing crew or create your own, chat in-crew, and pool your banked $MOON onto a shared crew leaderboard. Each crew has a captain who can manage the roster.

Crew Wars run on a weekly cycle. Members earn war points for the crew through play and by rallying. Each week your crew is matched against a rival; pull ahead and the crew unlocks a Victory Chest whose reward scales with your lead and your personal contribution. War points and the matchup reset every week.

Honest note: crew leaderboards and member contributions are real and server-tracked. In the current build the weekly rival opponent is simulated for pacing; true crew-vs-crew live PvP matchmaking is on the roadmap.

6. $MOON & Airdrop Points

The game uses two separate in-game scores:

  • $MOON — your spendable in-game balance, earned by banking profitable rounds. Used for upgrades, VIP and progression. It can be spent and lost.
  • Airdrop Points — your season qualification score, earned through sustained, legitimate play and quests. Points cannot be wagered or spent and are designed to reward real engagement over time.

How Airdrop Points are earned

A fraction of every profitable cash-out, plus quest completions, daily rewards, achievements, social tasks, and the Season Pass (+50,000 points) all add to your points total.

How to qualify (per cycle)

Qualification is deliberately about consistency, not one lucky run. A typical cycle target:

  • 10,000,000 points accumulated in the cycle
  • 20 active days of play
  • 5,000 taps
  • 100 cash-outs

👑 Elite status starts at 30,000,000 points and earns a larger share of the cycle pool. Thresholds are illustrative and may be tuned per cycle.

Both scores are off-chain in-game points today. They are not a cryptocurrency and carry no guaranteed value.

8. Proposed tokenomics

Most first-generation play-to-earn economies failed for one reason: they minted rewards faster than the game could absorb them and assumed growth would never stop. The $MOON model is built on the opposite principles — cap supply, release it slowly, and engineer real demand sinks into the game so the economy survives the quiet months, not just the hype. Everything below is proposed and illustrative; no token is offered or promised.

8.1 A two-layer model

The game separates the fun money from the real asset — the structure that keeps modern GameFi economies stable:

🎮 Game layer (off-chain, uncapped)

In-game $MOON balance and Airdrop Points are earned and spent inside the game — upgrades, VIP, rank, progression. They flow with seasons and are never a tradeable asset, so unlimited grinding can't inflate the token.

🪙 Token layer (on-chain, capped)

The proposed $MOON token is a fixed-supply on-chain asset. The game layer only ever qualifies you for a bounded share via airdrop snapshots — it cannot mint new tokens. Scarcity is protected by design.

8.2 Supply

Total supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000 $MOON — hard-capped, with no inflation mechanism and no ability to mint beyond the cap. The only direction supply moves is down, via burns (§8.7). There is no private sale and no venture allocation.

8.3 Allocation

Roughly two-thirds is community-directed — airdrops, ongoing play-to-earn, and referral/crew rewards:

🪂 Pre-launch airdrops (6 cycles)40%
🎮 Post-launch play-to-earn15%
🤝 Referrals & crews10%

8.4 Vesting & unlock schedule

Vesting stops anyone — team, treasury, or airdrop farmers — from dumping into thin early liquidity. Every allocation unlocks gradually, with cliffs and locks aligned to long-term commitment:

🎮 Post-launch P2E15%Released on a declining 4-year emission curve (§8.5). Never a flat, perpetual faucet.

8.5 Emission curve

The post-launch P2E pool (150M $MOON) is the only ongoing source of new circulating tokens, and it declines every year so rewards can't outrun demand: roughly 40% in Year 1, 30% in Year 2, 20% in Year 3, 10% in Year 4 — front-loaded to bootstrap the economy, then tapering toward a low baseline. After Year 4 the pool is effectively exhausted and the game runs on a recycled, circulating economy rather than fresh emissions.

8.6 The sustainability engine — sinks vs sources

A healthy economy keeps demand (sinks) at or above new supply (sources). Nearly every system that pays out tokens is matched by one that consumes them:

⬆️ Sources (supply in)

Airdrop claims (one-time, vested, capped) · declining P2E rewards · referral & crew milestones. All bounded, server-validated, none perpetual.

⬇️ Sinks (demand out)

Cosmetics marketplace · VIP, boosts & energy · tournament entry fees · permanent upgrades · staking locks · crew treasuries & wagered wars · a portion of every spend is burned.

8.7 Value accrual & deflation

  • 🔥 Buyback & burn — a share of marketplace fees and Telegram Stars revenue buys back and permanently burns $MOON, turning real product revenue into deflationary pressure.
  • 🔒 Staking — lock $MOON for boosts, energy and seasonal multipliers; staked tokens leave circulation.
  • 🏆 Utility demand — tournaments, marketplace, VIP, fee discounts and cross-app access all require $MOON, tying demand to active play.

Because supply is capped and burns are permanent, sustained sinks make the circulating supply deflationary over time — the opposite of the infinite-mint models that collapsed.

11. Fair play & anti-cheat

The economy is server-authoritative. Your client never reports its own balance — it only requests actions (cash out, buy an upgrade, claim a reward), and the server validates and applies every balance and point change. The sync endpoint saves cosmetic settings only, so a tampered client cannot inject fake totals.

Each request carries Telegram's cryptographically signed identity, verified server-side, so every action is tied to a real account. Cash-out payouts are capped to the most a player's tier could plausibly win in a round, rewards are idempotent (each credits at most once), and implausible jumps are flagged — flagged accounts can be reset or made ineligible for rewards and the airdrop. Leaderboards read directly from the server database, so they only ever contain server-validated scores, and every cycle snapshot comes from that same authoritative data.

Provably-fair rounds are next on the roadmap: a commit-reveal scheme where the server publishes a hash of each round's secret seed before you play and reveals the seed after, so the rug outcome is locked in advance and independently verifiable — the house can't rug you for winning.

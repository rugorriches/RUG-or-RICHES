Most play-to-earn economies failed for one reason: they printed rewards faster than the game could absorb them, and assumed growth would never stop. Modern, sustainable GameFi design does the opposite — it caps supply, releases it slowly, and builds real demand sinks into the game so the economy survives the quiet months, not just the hype. The $MOON model below is built on those principles.

1. A two-layer model

RUG OR RICHES separates the fun money from the real asset — the structure that keeps sustainable economies stable:

🎮 Game layer (off-chain, uncapped)

In-game $MOON balance and Airdrop Points are earned and spent inside the game. They power upgrades, VIP and rank, reset and flow with seasons, and are never a tradeable asset. This is where unlimited grinding lives — so it can't inflate the real token.

🪙 Token layer (on-chain, capped)

The proposed $MOON token is a fixed-supply, on-chain asset. The game layer only ever qualifies you for a bounded share of it via airdrop snapshots — it cannot mint new tokens. Scarcity is protected by design.

This is the dual-layer pattern modern GameFi uses to avoid the hyperinflation that collapsed first-generation play-to-earn tokens.

2. Supply

Total supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000 $MOON — hard-capped, with no inflation mechanism and no ability to mint beyond the cap. The only path supply ever moves is down, through burns (see §7). There is no private sale and no venture allocation; the economy is built for players first.

4. Vesting & unlock schedule

Vesting exists to stop anyone — team, treasury, or even airdrop farmers — from dumping into thin early liquidity. Every allocation unlocks gradually, with cliffs and locks aligned to long-term commitment:

Cliffs and linear vesting follow standard anti-dump practice: a 12-month team cliff acts as a loyalty lock, and gradual release keeps circulating supply predictable.

6. The sustainability engine — sinks vs sources

A healthy token economy keeps demand (sinks) at or above new supply (sources). The game is designed so nearly every system that pays out tokens is matched by a system that consumes them:

⬆️ Sources (supply in)

  • Airdrop claims (one-time, vested, capped)
  • Post-launch P2E rewards (declining)
  • Referral & crew milestone rewards

All bounded, all server-validated, none perpetual.

⬇️ Sinks (demand out)

  • Cosmetics & skins marketplace
  • VIP tiers, boosts & energy
  • Tournament entry fees & prize pools
  • Permanent upgrades
  • Staking locks (removes circulating supply)
  • Crew treasury deposits & wagered wars
  • A portion of every spend is burned

Sinks are embedded where players already want to spend — customization, status, competition and progression — so demand grows naturally with engagement instead of relying on new buyers.

7. Value accrual & deflation

🔥 Buyback & burn

A share of marketplace fees and Telegram Stars revenue is used to buy back $MOON and permanently burn it — turning real product revenue into deflationary pressure.

🔒 Staking

Lock $MOON for in-game boosts, energy and seasonal multipliers. Staked tokens leave circulation, rewarding holders who play the long game.

🏆 Utility demand

Tournaments, marketplace, VIP, fee discounts and cross-app access all require $MOON, tying token demand directly to active play.

Because supply is capped and burns are permanent, sustained sinks make the circulating supply deflationary over time — the opposite of the infinite-mint models that collapsed.

8. Liquidity & launch

  • Deep initial liquidity seeded from the 15% allocation at launch, with LP locked 12+ months to protect the market.
  • Claim portal opens after Cycle 6 against a snapshot of server-validated points — no off-chain trust required at distribution.
  • Phased listings, starting with a DEX and expanding as the ecosystem matures.
  • Transparent unlock calendar published so the community can see exactly what enters circulation and when.

9. Treasury & governance

The 10% treasury is multisig-controlled and reserved for liquidity support, listings, audits, partnerships and grants — with a long 48-month vest so it can never be drained quickly. Over time, governance-lite lets token holders vote on seasons, events, prize pools and feature priorities, gradually shifting direction to the community that built the game.
